    Approach
    Approach

    This is not the first prediction website/app for diabetes type II. Therefore, we elaborate here how it differs from other websites. Most diabetes predictors out there try to estimate the chance of getting diabetes with medical information such as BMI, blood sugar, blood pressure etc. (see e.g. Diabetes Check of Group Mutuel). Our approach also needs medical information (e.g. high blood pressure or heart disease) but it is easier to know if you have low blood pressure than the exact value of it. In addition to that we focus more on a person's lifestyle: Eating, drinking sugar-sweetened beverage and smoking habits are considered. So generally, the information the user of this website needs should be easily accessible. Our approach also might differ from other predictors in the technical aspects.

    Preprocessing
    Preprocessing

    Our original data comes from a survey done in Austin, Texas between the years 2015 and 2017 with 1'688 participants (Source: Austin Public Health Diabetes Self Management Education). First, we had to drop some columns of the dataset which were too specific for the USA. E.g. we dropped the insurance or the medical home category of the participants as the insurance system depends a lot on the country where you live in. Further, we dropped the variable "Carbohydrate Counting" as we believe that not too many people are doing it. Second, we had to clean the dataset (e.g. combining category "college" and "some college". Third, we put the participant's ages into bins: E.g. every participant in their twenties then belonged to the 20-29 bin.

    As an Artifical Intelligence can only handle numeric values we had to create dummy variables such that our AI could process the data:

    For an example see image below: In the original dataset there was the column Education Level which had four answers (1-8, 9-11, High School GED, College) but as the AI cannot interpret those values, we created four columns from this (dummy variables: Education Level_1-8, Education Level_9-11, Education Level_High School GED and Education Level_College) and put a 1 at the column/answer the participant selected and a 0 at the other columns. This was repeated for multiple columns.

    Further, we dropped rows which contained empty or invalid values (so called NaN values). In the end we then had 504 people with diabetes and 647 people without diabetes in the dataset.

    Artificial Intelligence
    Artificial Intelligence

    To train the AI we tried different models: First, linear models (e.g. logistic regression) then more advanced models (e.g. RandomForestClassifier). When we tried those models out we have seen that they unjustifiably misestimated the risk of getting diabetes of people with age between 60-69. Although, we tried out very positive behavior (e.g. never drinking sugar-sweetend beverages) entering 60-69 for the age always resulted in an assessment of having a high risk of diabetes. We came to the conclusion that the tree of the feature age must have a lot of weight in the RandomForestClassifier so that the diabetes risk is mainly decided by the age of the user. Therefore, we could not make use of it.

    In the end, we decided to train a Neural Network, more exactly a Multi-Layer-Perceptron. This is a Neural Network with more than one hidden layer (where the calculation of the weights and its decisions happen). We did a GridSearchCV to find the best hyperparameters of the MLP (click here for more information on GridSearchCV). The best results were given with a learning rate of 0.01, an alpha of 0.1 and the identity (linear) activation function. The activation function is used to calculate the output of a node in the Neural Network. The identity function is just f(x) = x, so there is no calculation needed. The learning rate controls the step-size in updating the weights of the nodes. Alpha is also called regularization term (in this case L2 regularization) and is intended to prevent overfitting (model is perfectly fitted on training data but is not able to generalize it for new data). For more information on these terms check the book called "Neural Network Methods for Natural Language Processing" of Yoav Goldberg (published in 2017).

    The accuracy this method achieves is nearly the same as in the logistic regression but the false assessment of the people in their sixties was gone. Overall, we achieve an accuracy of 69% percent (with a training/test split of 80/20 of the dataset) which means that in 31% of the cases we expect a false prediction of our predictor.


    Legal
    Privacy by design and Privacy by default

    Through our project, we wanted to illustrate privacy by design and privacy by default in a transparent way with a userfriendly approach from the very start of this project. Privacy by design is a concept where privacy protection considerations must be taken into account into the design of the product or service that will collect, process or use personal data. This means that privacy must be a concern of the developers from the beginning in order to integrate it into the structure of the service (EU GDPR info). For example, at the beginning of our project we had to take into consideration the principle of minimization of personal data, which requires that the collection of personal data is strictly limited to what is necessary for the realization of the service. No superfluous data should be collected if it has no use for the service.

    Privacy by default is the consequence of privacy by design, which implies that from the conception of the service or product, the highest level of privacy protection is implemented and applicable by default. This means that without any intervention from the user, all available measures to protect personal data and limit its collection must be ensured (EUROPEAN DATA PROTECTION SUPERVISOR). For example, when creating a UDP (UZH Diabetes Predictor) user account, the user is confronted with a box giving him the necessary instructions to understand the data he is about to disclose. This is an application of privacy by default insofar as access to personal data (email address, answers to the risk predictor test) is subject to the user's prior authorization, without which the site will not grant access to the information.

    Research Purpose
    Research Purpose

    The Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP) contains two specific regulations concerning the processing of personal data for research purposes, one for the private sector art. 13 para. 2 e FADP and the other one that concerns the federal bodies only art. 22 FADP. Since we are a group of private persons and in no event we are federal bodies, we are subject to a less stringent legal regime if the following conditions are met: Firstly, the purpose of the processing of personal data does not relate to persons. That means that the identity of the person whose data is processed plays no role for the processing and anonymized or at least pseudonymized data could also be used to achieve the same purpose. This is the case for our project where the processing concerns only the users’: email adress, username, password age, gender, educational level, diabetes status, heart disease, blood pressure, smoking, diabetes education, diabetes knowledge, fruit and vegetable consumption per week, sweet beverages consumption per week and physical exercise per week. . Secondly, the results of the research are published in a form that does not allow the identification of the persons concerned. As we guarantee, the results are not concerning the individuals taking the test at all, and our goal as explained above is completely else. We found our data on Austin Public Health Diabetes Self Management Education and our algorithm is set and will not be modified. Therefore big data and personal private information related to the different questions in the test would be irrelevant to collect for us. The art. 13 par. 2 let. e FADP therefore refers to situations in which non-anonymized data as the one on our website (given the email address linked to the information) are used for purposes that do not relate to individuals, which excludes research that is directly directed at individuals. "On the other hand there is the anonymous access to UDP, where no data is collected. Therefore the unregistered users are not concerned by the FADP. The researcher must use anonymized data within the limits of the project's possibilities, it refers to the principle of the art. 4 par. 2 FADP which cites the principle of good faith and proportionality i.e. without any possibility of attributing data to an identified or identifiable person or at the cost of excessive efforts. In our case, however, this was not technically possible because of the means of retrieving one's password for the user account, which is only possible via e-mail. Since participation in a project with an educational purpose like ours is optional, the data subject must also consent to the processing of his or her data and has at least the right to object. It should be noted that this consent is indispensable in the case of processing of data subject to professional secrecy, such as medical secrecy art. 321bis of the Swiss Criminal Code (SCC), article that refers to the LRH. This privilege reserved for research projects does not, however, dispense with the need to provide information about the data processing. Thus, the data subject must be given all objective and complete information on the planned data processing in advance “at the latest when the data is stored or if the data is not stored, on its first disclosure to a third party” on according to art. 14 par. 3 FADP. The requirements to be met by the researcher can vary, since each research is seeking different purposes and therefore are subject to each individual case, which must be examined in the light of the context of the research and the specificity of the concrete cases (FANTI 2017). The researcher, as the person responsible for data protection in the processing of personal data, will comply with the following points in particular in the context of his research project.

    Conclusion
    Conclusion

    Overall we think we can raise awareness on the diabetes II-type illness by this website, by adding the component of the people's lifestyle choices as an aspect of the illness. Nevertheless, we are aware that there are AIs out there which perform much better than our predictor e.g. by using more data.
